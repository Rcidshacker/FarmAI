## OUTPUT 2: SYNTHESIS REPORT

### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Mealybugs (Hemiptera: Pseudococcidae) represent a significant threat to horticultural crops, particularly Custard Apple (*Annona squamosa*) and Durian. This analysis synthesizes data from 8 documents covering species biology, specific infestation of custard apples, and management strategies.

**Key Findings:**

1. **Dominant Species:** The primary threats to custard apple are *Dysmicoccus neobrevipes* (Gray pineapple mealybug) and *Maconellicoccus hirsutus* (Pink hibiscus mealybug).
2. **Biological Vulnerability:** These pests develop rapidly (55-68 days) in tropical conditions (26-32¬∞C). Their waxy hydrophobic coating complicates chemical control, making enzyme-producing entomopathogenic fungi (EPF) a vital control avenue.
3. **Economic Impact:** Unchecked infestations lead to yield losses between 30-60%. Severe infestation results in fruit drop, shriveling, and sooty mold development which renders fruit unmarketable.
4. **Control Consensus:** Azadirachtin (10,000 ppm) and specific fungal isolates (*Metarhizium* and *Lecanicillium*) offer high efficacy (>90% mortality or significant population reduction) and are superior to untreated controls.

### DETAILED BIOLOGY & ECOLOGY

Mealybugs are soft-bodied, sap-sucking insects characterized by a white waxy coating.

* **Life Cycle:** Females lay 50-600 eggs in cottony masses [Doc 1, Doc 3]. However, *D. neobrevipes* completes its life cycle in 55-68 days at 26-32¬∞C [Doc 2].
* **Ant Mutualism:** A significant barrier to control is the mutualistic relationship with ants (*Camponotus compressus*, *Anoplolepis longipes*). Ants protect mealybugs from predators in exchange for honeydew [Doc 3, Doc 6].
* **Defense Mechanism:** The waxy cuticle is hydrophobic. Research indicates that EPF virulence is correlated with the production of lipase and protease enzymes, which degrade this wax layer [Doc 2].

### CUSTARD APPLE SPECIFIC SECTION

**Vulnerability:**
Custard apples are susceptible from fruit set (March in Thailand/India) through harvest (June-October). Mealybugs prefer the "areolar grooves" of the fruit, making physical removal difficult [Doc 2].

**Management Recommendations:**

1. **Chemical/Botanical:** Azadirachtin 10,000 ppm (0.003%) is the most effective botanical treatment, reducing populations to ~7.8 bugs/fruit compared to ~28 in controls [Doc 5].
2. **Biological:** *Lecanicillium lecanii* (1.15 WP) is statistically on par with Azadirachtin for suppression [Doc 5]. *Metarhizium* isolate NMMet_SS2/2 showed 96.67% mortality in lab trials [Doc 2].
3. **Integrated Strategy (IPM):** JAU [Doc 7] recommends a rotation:
    * Spray 1: *Beauveria bassiana* (1.15 WP) at initiation.
    * Spray 2: Fenobucarb 50 EC (0.1%) 15 days later.

* **Detection:** E-nose sensors can detect infestation odors before visual signs appear (emerging tech).
